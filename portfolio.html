Reference Photo Guide

Quality Matters

The quality of your reference photo is vital to helping me capture your pet on paper. Here's what makes a great reference photo:

Ideal head shot
Ideal head shot

✓ Ideal Photos Have:

  • Natural sunlight (best lighting)
  • Clear, visible details of the face
  • Complete image of face and torso
  • Taken at eye level and close proximity
  • Visible pupils and light reflection in eyes
Blurry/poor lighting
Blurry/poor lighting

✗ Avoid These:

  • Blurry or distant images
  • Poor lighting or shadows on the face
  • Photos taken at awkward angles
  • Overly filtered or edited images
  • Flash photography (creates unnatural reflections)
Candid snapshot
Awkward angle

Client Feedback

Artistic Notes

This portrait required over 30 hours of work, with special attention paid to recreating the golden retriever's distinctive layered fur texture. The eyes were built up with 15 different shades to achieve depth and realism.
